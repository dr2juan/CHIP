# Grant: ASN — School Nutrition / USDA School Meals

**Focus:** Pork acceptability and nutrition outcomes within the National School Lunch Program (NSLP) at El Paso border schools.

---

## Key Papers

### E021 ★ — Spill MK et al. 2024 | *JAMA Network Open*
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2024.24082

**Use in:** Significance, Background, Specific Aims

> "Universal free school meals consistently increased meal participation and improved child dietary quality — making elimination of cost barriers the highest-impact policy lever for school nutrition programs."

- Most recent systematic review on universal free school meals (2024)
- Shows NSLP-based interventions have measurable impact on dietary quality
- Strongest opening citation for the school meals significance argument

---

### E022 ★ — Kinderknecht K et al. 2020 | *JAMA*
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1001/jama.2020.9517

**Use in:** Significance, Background, Policy Context

> "After the Healthy Hunger-Free Kids Act, NSLP participants' dietary quality scores improved significantly — proving federal nutrition standards can measurably improve what children eat at school."

- High-impact JAMA paper on federal policy (HHFKA) + dietary quality
- Establishes evidence base that NSLP-level policy changes work
- Use when making the case that NSLP menu changes have real dietary impact

---

### E023 — Rains C et al. 2019 | *Translational Behavioral Medicine*
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1093/tbm/ibz092

**Use in:** Innovation, Approach, Precedent for grant-funded model

> "USDA farm-to-school grants were associated with improved nutrition behaviors in low-income children — supporting grant-funded school food programming as a viable strategy for under-resourced communities."

- Establishes USDA grant-funded school food programs as evidence-based
- Useful for framing the ASN grant itself as following a proven funding model
- Especially relevant for border/low-income school context

---

### Supporting: E011 — Greatorex Brooks & McInerney 2023 | *J Nutritional Science*
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1017/jns.2023.81

> "Fruit and vegetable prescription programs consistently improve produce intake across diverse populations — establishing produce prescription as an evidence-based model for food-insecure communities."

- Links to broader nutrition intervention evidence base

---

### Supporting: E013 — Schier et al. 2024 | *Nutrition Reviews*
**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1093/nutrit/nuad125

> "Food provision interventions for diabetes management achieve significant glycemic improvements — demonstrating that addressing food access is as essential as clinical management for diabetes control."

- Food-as-medicine framing; useful if grant connects school meals to chronic disease prevention
