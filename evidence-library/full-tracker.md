## Pillar 1 — Cardiometabolic & Chronic Disease

### E001 — CVD Disparities in Hispanic Subgroups
- **Authors:** Serpa et al.
- **Year:** 2025 | **Journal:** American Heart Journal
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ahj.2025.03.002
- **Key Finding:** CVD risk and outcomes vary significantly across Hispanic subgroups — Mexican-Americans, Puerto Ricans, and Cuban-Americans face distinct profiles; one-size-fits-all approaches miss important variation.
- **Quotable Stat:** "CVD risk and outcomes vary significantly across Hispanic subgroups — demonstrating that disaggregated data and subgroup-specific interventions are essential for reducing cardiovascular disparities."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 1 significance; health disparities framing

### E002 — Sleep Disorders → HTN/Diabetes (HCHS/SOL)
- **Authors:** Li et al.
- **Year:** 2021 | **Journal:** AJRCCM
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1164/rccm.201912-2330OC
- **Key Finding:** Sleep-disordered breathing independently associated with incident hypertension and diabetes in the Hispanic Community Health Study/Study of Latinos (HCHS/SOL).
- **Quotable Stat:** "In the Hispanic Community Health Study, sleep-disordered breathing was independently associated with incident hypertension and diabetes — linking a modifiable risk factor to cardiometabolic outcomes in Latinos."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 1; sleep-cardiometabolic link

### E003 — CVD Risk in Latino Farmworkers (Meta-analysis)
- **Authors:** Mendez
- **Year:** 2024 | **Journal:** Hispanic Health Care International
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1177/15404153241302253
- **Key Finding:** Meta-analysis confirms elevated CVD risk among Latino farmworkers; occupational factors (heat, physical demand, limited healthcare) compound traditional risk factors.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Meta-analysis confirms Latino farmworkers face elevated CVD risk driven by the convergence of occupational exposures, limited healthcare access, and social determinants — a population requiring targeted intervention."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 1; farmworker CVD; occupational-cardiometabolic intersection

### E004 ★ — Healthy Fit CHW Model, El Paso (CHIP Track Record)
- **Authors:** Brown, Vasquez et al.
- **Year:** 2018 | **Journal:** Preventing Chronic Disease (CDC)
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5888/pcd15.170347
- **Key Finding:** Community health worker–led Healthy Fit program in El Paso achieved significant improvements in blood pressure and physical activity in a predominantly Hispanic population.
- **Quotable Stat:** "The Healthy Fit CHW-led program in El Paso achieved significant blood pressure reductions in a predominantly Hispanic community — establishing a replicable model for CHW-based chronic disease prevention at the border."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 1 + Track Record; Denise Vasquez (current CHIP Research Coordinator) is co-author

### E005 — Hypertension/Diabetes Screening at Laundromats
- **Authors:** McCann et al.
- **Year:** 2025 | **Journal:** American Journal of Health Promotion
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1177/08901171251375071
- **Key Finding:** Laundromat-based HTN/diabetes screening in San Antonio reached predominantly Latino adults who had not recently engaged with the healthcare system; high rates of undiagnosed hypertension detected.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Laundromat-based screening in San Antonio detected high rates of previously undiagnosed hypertension among Latino adults — validating non-traditional community venues as essential access points for chronic disease detection."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 1; community-based screening; non-traditional venues

## Pillar 2 — Environmental & Occupational Health

### E006 — Extreme Heat: Occupational Risks (Annual Review)
- **Authors:** Gibb et al.
- **Year:** 2024 | **Journal:** Annual Review of Public Health
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-publhealth-060222-034715
- **Key Finding:** Comprehensive review of extreme heat as an occupational hazard; outdoor and agricultural workers face highest risk; climate change is expanding the threat season and geographic range.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Extreme heat is now one of the leading occupational hazards, with outdoor and agricultural workers facing disproportionate risk — a threat that is expanding in both duration and geography due to climate change."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 2; NIOSH grants — broad significance

### E007 — Occupational Heat Exposure Limits (CDC/NIOSH, MMWR)
- **Authors:** Tustin et al. (CDC/NIOSH)
- **Year:** 2018 | **Journal:** MMWR
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.15585/mmwr.mm6726a1
- **Key Finding:** NIOSH/CDC analysis of heat exposure thresholds; establishes surveillance data on occupational heat illness and reinforces the need for updated exposure limits.
- **Quotable Stat:** "CDC/NIOSH surveillance data confirm that current occupational heat exposure limits are insufficient — heat-related illness continues to occur at recommended threshold levels, demanding updated standards."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 2; NIOSH grants — regulatory/policy context

### E008 — Heat-Related Illness Risk Factors in CA Workers
- **Authors:** Heinzerling et al.
- **Year:** 2020 | **Journal:** American Journal of Industrial Medicine
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1002/ajim.23191
- **Key Finding:** In California agricultural workers, HRI risk factors include high physical exertion, inadequate hydration, lack of acclimatization, and immigrant/undocumented status limiting access to protections.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Undocumented status, inadequate acclimatization, and high exertion are independent HRI risk factors in California agricultural workers — a profile that mirrors conditions for border-region outdoor workers."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 2; NIOSH and VALE+Tú grants

### E009 ★ — HIPP Texas 9-Year Program (UTHealth SWCOEH)
- **Authors:** Perkison et al.
- **Year:** 2024 | **Journal:** Journal of Occupational and Environmental Medicine
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1097/JOM.0000000000003051
- **Key Finding:** The UTHealth SWCOEH Heat Illness Prevention Program (HIPP) in Texas demonstrated sustained reductions in heat-related illness over 9 years; worker training and environmental monitoring were key.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Over 9 years, the UTHealth SWCOEH HIPP program achieved sustained reductions in heat-related illness in Texas workers — establishing a proven model for occupational heat prevention directly relevant to CHIP's SWCOEH-funded work."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 2 + Track Record; UTHealth SWCOEH direct connection

### E010 — Asthma + Air Pollutants, US-Mexico Border
- **Authors:** Carrillo et al.
- **Year:** 2017 | **Journal:** Environmental Research
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.envres.2017.11.057
- **Key Finding:** Increased asthma prevalence and severity in border communities associated with elevated PM2.5, ozone, and transboundary air pollution; children and outdoor workers most affected.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Elevated PM2.5 and ozone at the US-Mexico border are significantly associated with increased asthma rates — highlighting the compounding air quality burden facing El Paso communities."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 2; border air quality; supports heat+ozone grant rationale

## Pillar 3 — Food Systems & Nutrition

### E011 — Fruit/Vegetable Prescription Scoping Review
- **Authors:** Greatorex Brooks & McInerney
- **Year:** 2023 | **Journal:** Journal of Nutritional Science
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1017/jns.2023.81
- **Key Finding:** Scoping review of FV prescription programs (like Veggie Scripts) found consistent associations with improved fruit and vegetable intake; implementation models vary widely.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Fruit and vegetable prescription programs consistently improve produce intake across diverse populations — establishing produce prescription as an evidence-based intervention model for food insecure communities."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 3; La Semilla / Veggie Scripts track record

### E012 — Pediatric FV Prescription + Food Security
- **Authors:** Ridberg et al.
- **Year:** 2018 | **Journal:** Journal of Nutrition Education and Behavior
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jneb.2018.08.003
- **Key Finding:** Pediatric produce prescription programs reduced food insecurity and improved fruit and vegetable intake in low-income families; pediatrician referrals increased uptake.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Pediatric produce prescriptions reduced food insecurity and improved fruit/vegetable intake in low-income families — making clinician referral a powerful lever for nutrition-based food security interventions."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 3; pediatric nutrition; food security

### E013 — Food Provision + Diabetes Outcomes (Review)
- **Authors:** Schier et al.
- **Year:** 2024 | **Journal:** Nutrition Reviews
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1093/nutrit/nuad125
- **Key Finding:** Review of food provision interventions for diabetes management found significant improvements in glycemic control when healthy food was provided alongside diabetes care.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Food provision interventions for diabetes management achieve significant glycemic improvements — demonstrating that addressing food access is as essential as clinical management for diabetes control."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 3; diabetes management; food as medicine

### E014 — Local Food Systems Systematic Review
- **Authors:** Garrity et al.
- **Year:** 2024 | **Journal:** Advances in Nutrition
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.advnut.2023.100156
- **Key Finding:** Systematic review found local food systems interventions improve food access, diet quality, and community economic outcomes; effects strongest in food insecure and rural/border communities.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Local food systems interventions improve diet quality and food access most strongly in food-insecure and rural communities — directly supporting CHIP's local food systems work in the El Paso border region."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 3; local food systems; La Semilla partnerships

### E015 — Prescription Produce + Counseling
- **Authors:** Battle et al.
- **Year:** 2025 | **Journal:** Translational Behavioral Medicine
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1093/tbm/ibae067
- **Key Finding:** Combined produce prescription + nutrition counseling achieved greater dietary improvements than either alone; integration with healthcare settings enhanced uptake and retention.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Produce prescription combined with nutrition counseling achieved significantly greater dietary improvements than either alone — supporting an integrated clinical + community model for CHIP's produce prescription programs."
- **Grant Use:** Pillar 3; Veggie Scripts model; integration with clinical care

## Grant-Specific Entries

### E016 — Heat + Air Pollution Co-exposure: 482 Cities
- **Authors:** Rai M et al.
- **Year:** 2023 | **Journal:** Environmental International
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.envint.2023.107825
- **Key Finding:** In 482 cities across 24 countries, combined heat and air pollution exposure was associated with significantly greater cardiorespiratory mortality than either hazard alone.
- **Quotable Stat:** "In 482 cities across 24 countries, heat combined with air pollution significantly exceeded mortality risk from either hazard alone — supporting integrated heat-ozone monitoring for occupational settings."
- **Grant Use:** NIOSH pilot grant (heat+ozone) — Significance; Specific Aims; Innovation

### E017 — Concurrent Heatwave + High Ozone: Mortality
- **Authors:** Oh et al.
- **Year:** 2026 | **Journal:** Environmental Research
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.envres.2026.125027
- **Key Finding:** Concurrent heatwave and high-ozone days were associated with significantly higher mortality in Korean cities than either hazard in isolation.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Concurrent heatwave + high ozone days carried significantly higher mortality risk than either hazard alone — establishing compound exposure as a distinct, more dangerous risk category."
- **Grant Use:** NIOSH pilot grant (heat+ozone) — Significance; Background

### E018 ★ — Wearable Devices for Heat-Exposed Outdoor Workers (Scoping Review)
- **Authors:** Friedrich et al.
- **Year:** 2025 | **Journal:** BMC Public Health
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1186/s12889-025-24262-2
- **Key Finding:** Scoping review found wearable interventions for heat-exposed outdoor workers are feasible but rigorous effectiveness evidence is limited; cost, comfort, and data privacy are key barriers.
- **Quotable Stat:** "A scoping review found wearable interventions for heat-exposed outdoor workers are feasible but effectiveness evidence remains limited — highlighting the gap this grant aims to fill."
- **Grant Use:** NIOSH wearables co-PI grant — Significance; Innovation; Specific Aims; Gap Statement

### E019 — Core Temperature from Heart Rate (Wearable Methods)
- **Authors:** Buller MJ et al.
- **Year:** 2017 | **Journal:** Journal of Applied Physiology
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1152/japplphysiol.00353.2017
- **Key Finding:** Wearable sensor algorithm accurately estimated core body temperature from heart rate, enabling real-time detection of thermal-work strain without invasive measurement.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Wearable physiological monitoring can accurately estimate core body temperature from heart rate — enabling real-time detection of dangerous heat strain in workers without invasive measurement."
- **Grant Use:** NIOSH wearables grant — Innovation; Approach; Preliminary Data

### E020 — Workers' Perceptions of Wearable Heat Sensors
- **Authors:** Sugg MM et al.
- **Year:** 2019 | **Journal:** International Journal of Biometeorology
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s00484-019-01833-8
- **Key Finding:** Outdoor workers generally accepted wearable sensors for heat monitoring but raised significant concerns about privacy and employer surveillance of monitoring data.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Outdoor workers accepted wearable heat sensors but cited privacy and employer surveillance as key concerns — critical community engagement considerations for wearable monitoring grant design."
- **Grant Use:** NIOSH wearables grant — Innovation; Human Subjects; Community Engagement

### E021 ★ — Universal Free School Meals: Systematic Review
- **Authors:** Spill MK et al.
- **Year:** 2024 | **Journal:** JAMA Network Open
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2024.24082
- **Key Finding:** Universal free school meals consistently associated with increased meal participation and improved dietary quality; strongest effects in low-income schools.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Universal free school meals consistently increased meal participation and improved child dietary quality — making elimination of cost barriers the highest-impact policy lever for school nutrition programs."
- **Grant Use:** ASN school nutrition grant — Significance; Background; Specific Aims

### E022 ★ — Healthy Hunger-Free Kids Act + NSLP Dietary Quality

- **Quotable Stat:** "After the Healthy Hunger-Free Kids Act, NSLP participants' dietary quality scores improved significantly — proving federal nutrition standards can measurably improve what children eat at school."
- **Grant Use:** ASN school nutrition grant — Significance; Background; Policy Context

### E023 — Farm-to-School Grants in Low-Income Communities

- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1093/tbm/ibz092
- **Key Finding:** USDA farm-to-school education grants were associated with improved fruit and vegetable intake and nutrition knowledge in low-income children.
- **Quotable Stat:** "USDA farm-to-school grants were associated with improved nutrition behaviors in low-income children — supporting grant-funded school food programming as a viable strategy for under-resourced communities."

### E024 — Systemic Racism and Undocumented Latino Migrant Laborers
- **Authors:** Salinas JL, Salinas M
- **Year:** 2022 | **Journal:** Journal of Migration and Health
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jmh.2022.100106
- **Key Finding:** Systemic racism structures work conditions for undocumented Latino migrant laborers, exposing them to occupational hazards while denying access to protective equipment, healthcare, and federal relief.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Undocumented Latino migrant laborers were designated 'essential workers' while systemic racism left them without workplace protections or healthcare access — a structural gap requiring targeted occupational health research."
- **Grant Use:** NIMHD VALE+Tú — Significance; Health Disparities Framework; Background

### E025 — Peer-Led Training for Latino Construction Day Laborers
- **Authors:** Williams Q et al.
- **Year:** 2010 | **Journal:** Journal of Safety Research
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jsr.2010.02.009
- **Key Finding:** Peer-led participatory H&S training for Latino construction day laborers significantly improved PPE use; 66% of participants shared safety information with co-workers.
- **Quotable Stat:** "After peer-led participatory training, Latino day laborers significantly increased PPE use — and 66% shared safety information with coworkers, amplifying reach beyond the initial cohort."
- **Grant Use:** NIMHD VALE+Tú — Innovation; Approach; CBPR Methods; Track Record

### E026 — The Jornalero: Healthcare Access for Day Laborers
- **Authors:** Leclere OA, López RA
- **Year:** 2012 | **Journal:** Journal of Immigrant and Minority Health
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1007/s10903-011-9516-z
- **Key Finding:** Latino immigrant day laborers (jornaleros) in southern California were largely undocumented with virtually no healthcare resources beyond emergency services; worked in unmonitored, unsafe conditions.
- **Quotable Stat:** "Latino jornaleros work in occupations that are 'unmonitored, unprotected, and often unsafe' with no healthcare beyond emergency rooms — illustrating the structural gap in occupational health coverage for this population."
- **Grant Use:** NIMHD VALE+Tú — Significance; Background; Health Disparities

### E027 — Lancet Countdown 2024: 512 Billion Labor Hours Lost to Heat
- **Authors:** Romanello M et al.
- **Year:** 2024 | **Journal:** The Lancet
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1016/S0140-6736(24)01822-1
- **Key Finding:** In 2023, heat exposure led to a record loss of 512 billion potential labor hours globally (worth $835 billion); low-income countries bore 7.6% of GDP in losses; heat mortality for adults 65+ rose 167%.
- **Quotable Stat:** "In 2023, heat exposure cost the world a record 512 billion labor hours worth $835 billion in lost income — with low-income countries bearing 7.6% of GDP in losses, making this a clear equity and economic issue."
- **Grant Use:** NIOSH pilot grant; NIMHD VALE+Tú — Significance; Broader Impacts; Economic Justification
